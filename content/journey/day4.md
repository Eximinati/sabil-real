# Day 4 - Mercy Is Greater Than Your Shame

## Arrival moment

Breathe gently.
You do not return to Allah because you are flawless.
You return because He is Merciful.

"Do not despair of the mercy of Allah."

## Opening reflection

Shame can make the heart hide.
It tells you to wait until you are better before turning back.
Islam teaches the opposite:
turn back now, and healing begins there.

## Seerah moment

After the people of Ta'if rejected and harmed him, the Prophet (peace be upon him) did not harden into bitterness.
He turned to Allah with vulnerability and hope.

Pain did not end his mercy.
It deepened his dependence on Allah.

## Quran reflection

Read slowly and let these words settle.

39:53
93:5

## Tafsir support

The Quran does not deny your mistakes.
It denies hopelessness.
Allah's mercy is not permission to stay stuck; it is permission to return.

## Hadith connection

The Prophet (peace be upon him) said: "Allah is more joyful at the repentance of His servant than one who finds his lost camel in the desert."

## Private reflection

What part of your story feels hardest to bring to Allah?
What would sincere return look like for you this week?

## Tiny action

After Isha tonight, ask Allah for forgiveness in your own words for one specific thing.

## Closing moment

Mercy is not far from you.
Take one step back to Allah, and keep walking gently.
