# Day 8 - Trust Allah With What You Cannot Control

## Arrival moment

Settle your breath.
Not everything in your hands was meant to stay there.

"Whoever relies upon Allah - He is enough for them."

## Opening reflection

Control can feel like safety.
But often it leaves the heart exhausted.
Tawakkul does not mean doing nothing.
It means doing your part, then trusting Allah with what you cannot hold.

## Seerah moment

During Hijrah, the Prophet (peace be upon him) planned carefully, chose routes wisely, and still placed full trust in Allah.
Trust was not passivity.
It was effort with surrendered outcomes.

## Quran reflection

Read these verses with openness.

65:3
3:159

## Tafsir support

Reliance in Allah is strongest when joined to sincere action.
You tie your camel, then trust.
You plan, then release your heart from panic.

## Hadith connection

The Prophet (peace be upon him) said: "If you relied upon Allah with true reliance, He would provide for you as He provides for birds."

## Private reflection

Where are you holding too tightly today?
What would surrender with responsibility look like in that area?

## Tiny action

Name one worry in a sentence and end it with: "Allah is enough for me."

## Closing moment

You are allowed to breathe.
Do what you can, and place the rest with Allah.
