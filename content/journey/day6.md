# Day 6 - Your Dua Is Heard

## Arrival moment

Come quietly.
No polished words are required.

"Call upon Me; I will respond to you."

## Opening reflection

Sometimes unanswered dua feels like silence.
But in Islam, dua is never wasted.
It is worship, closeness, and trust - even before outcomes appear.

## Seerah moment

At Badr, the Prophet (peace be upon him) raised his hands and made heartfelt dua with complete dependence on Allah.
He prayed with urgency, humility, and certainty.

Dua was not his last resort.
It was his first refuge.

## Quran reflection

Read slowly, then sit in stillness.

40:60
2:186

## Tafsir support

Response to dua may come as immediate relief, delayed wisdom, redirected good, or protection from unseen harm.
Allah's response is never neglect; it is mercy with knowledge.

## Hadith connection

The Prophet (peace be upon him) said: "Dua is worship."

## Private reflection

What dua have you almost stopped making?
What would it mean to ask Allah again with hope?

## Tiny action

Write one dua in your notes app or journal, and make it after each prayer today.

## Closing moment

Your voice is not lost with Allah.
Ask again. Ask gently. Ask with hope.
Return tomorrow.
