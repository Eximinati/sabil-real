# Day 12 - Gratitude in the Ordinary

## Arrival moment

Pause and look around slowly.
Mercy is often quiet.

"If you are grateful, I will surely increase you."

## Opening reflection

Gratitude in Islam is not forced positivity.
It is truthful noticing.
A heart that notices mercy becomes more alive to Allah.

## Seerah moment

The Prophet (peace be upon him) thanked Allah deeply in daily acts - food, companionship, safety, prayer.
His gratitude was embodied, not abstract.

## Quran reflection

Read with a noticing heart.

14:7
55:13

## Tafsir support

Shukr is done by the tongue, the heart, and the limbs:
remembering the blessing,
thanking Allah,
and using blessings in ways He loves.

## Hadith connection

The Prophet (peace be upon him) said: "Who does not thank people has not thanked Allah."

## Private reflection

What mercy in your life have you started to overlook?
Who deserves sincere thanks from you today?

## Tiny action

Send one genuine message of gratitude to someone who made your life easier.

## Closing moment

Gratitude softens the heart and expands hope.
Keep noticing Allah's gifts.
