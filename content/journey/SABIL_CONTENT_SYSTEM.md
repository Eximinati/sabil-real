# Sabil Content System (Phase 3)

## Purpose

This system exists to help Sabil scale without losing its soul.

Sabil is a guided spiritual journey, not a course platform.
Every day should feel like: "You are welcome here. Walk gently toward Allah."

---

## 1) Sabil Writing Philosophy

### Tone
- Gentle, sincere, and calm
- Human before instructional
- Beginner-safe without diluting spiritual depth

### Emotional goals
- Safety before challenge
- Hope before correction
- Sincerity before performance
- Quiet desire to return tomorrow

### Pacing philosophy
- Alternate guidance and stillness
- Avoid long uninterrupted information blocks
- Use transitions that soften emotional movement
- End calmer than the beginning

### Teaching philosophy
- Teach from lived meaning before technical terminology
- Explain Islam as invitation, not confrontation
- Keep Quran and Seerah central
- Keep tafsir supportive and progressively revealed

### Transformation philosophy
- Growth is gradual, merciful, and relational
- One sincere step per day is enough
- Goal is closeness to Allah, not achievement psychology

### What Sabil avoids
- Shame or guilt pressure
- Preachy, harsh, or sectarian tone
- Corporate or productivity language
- Assuming prior Arabic/scholarly knowledge

### Good vs bad tone examples

Good:
- "Take a quiet breath. Let this verse meet you where you are."
- "You are not behind. Begin gently from today."

Bad:
- "If you were serious, you would already be doing this daily."
- "Complete this task to keep your momentum and growth."

---

## 2) Canonical Day Structure (8-15 min)

1. Arrival moment (0.5-1.5 min)
   - Emotional purpose: settle and welcome
2. Opening reflection (1-2 min)
   - Emotional purpose: prepare the heart
3. Seerah moment (1-2 min)
   - Emotional purpose: human Prophetic resonance
4. Quran reflection (2-4 min)
   - Emotional purpose: direct encounter with revelation
5. Tafsir insight (1-2 min)
   - Emotional purpose: contextual support, optional
6. Hadith connection (0.5-1.5 min)
   - Emotional purpose: mercy and closeness reinforcement
7. Reflection prompt (1-3 min)
   - Emotional purpose: private honesty before Allah
8. Tiny action (0.5-1 min)
   - Emotional purpose: one lived sincere step
9. Peaceful close (0.5-1.5 min)
   - Emotional purpose: hope and gentle return

Transition philosophy:
- noise -> presence -> meaning -> revelation -> response -> trust

---

## 3) Emotional Pacing Rules

- Start with stillness, not instruction.
- Raise intensity around Quran and reflection.
- Add breathing room after heavy ideas.
- Never stack dense Seerah + dense tafsir + legal detail.
- Keep tiny action small and non-pressuring.
- Close with emotional downshift and hope.

---

## 4) Reflection Prompt Guidelines

Good reflection prompts:
- Open, spiritually grounded, emotionally specific
- Compassionate in tone
- Invite honesty, not performance

Avoid prompts that sound:
- clinical/therapy-led
- corporate self-help
- productivity/optimization-focused
- guilt-heavy

Example prompts:
- "What burden have you been carrying silently lately?"
- "Where do you most need Allah's mercy this week?"

---

## 5) Seerah Usage Rules

Use:
- One resonant Prophetic moment per day
- Human scenes: uncertainty, patience, trust, mercy
- A clear emotional bridge to the reader's life

Avoid:
- Timeline dumping
- Trivia-heavy narration
- Academic biography tone detached from lived meaning

---

## 6) Quran Integration Standards

- Verse density: usually 1-5 verses/day
- Frame verses emotionally before showing them
- Keep Arabic visually central and spacious
- Keep translation clear and calm
- Use progressive tafsir reveal (condensed-first)

The Quran should be encountered, not consumed.

---

## 7) What Not To Do (Anti-Patterns)

- Information overload
- Sectarian debates
- Guilt-driven motivation
- Achievement psychology
- Excessive jargon
- Harsh correction tone
- Overwhelming legalism
- Over-academic abstraction

---

## 8) Lightweight Content QA (Before Publish)

Ask:
1. Is this emotionally safe for a struggling beginner?
2. Does it flow like one journey?
3. Is pacing calm with breathing room?
4. Is Quran spiritually central?
5. Is Seerah human and relevant?
6. Is tafsir supportive and not overwhelming?
7. Does reflection invite sincerity without guilt?
8. Is language beginner-accessible?
9. Is the tiny action gentle and realistic?
10. Does the close leave the reader calmer and hopeful?

If any answer is "no", revise before publishing.
