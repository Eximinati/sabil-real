# Day 4 - Mercy Is Greater Than Your Shame

## Arrival moment

Breathe gently.
You do not return to Allah because you are flawless.
You return because He is Merciful.

"Do not despair of mercy of Allah."

## Opening reflection

Shame can make heart hide.
It whispers: "Fix yourself first, then return."
Islam teaches opposite.
Return now, and healing begins there.

## Seerah moment

After people of Ta'if rejected and harmed him, Prophet (peace be upon him) did not harden into bitterness.
He turned to Allah with vulnerability and hope.

Pain did not end his mercy.
Pain deepened his dependence on Allah.

## Quran reflection

Read slowly and let words settle.

39:53
93:5

## Tafsir support

Quran does not deny your mistakes.
It denies hopelessness.
Allah's mercy is not permission to stay stuck.
It is permission to return.

## Hadith connection

Prophet (peace be upon him) said: "Allah is more joyful at repentance of His servant than one who finds lost camel in desert."

## Private reflection

What part of your story feels hardest to bring to Allah?
What would sincere return look like this week?

## Tiny action

After Isha tonight, ask Allah for forgiveness in your own words for one specific thing.

## Closing moment

Mercy is not far from you.
Take one step back to Allah, and keep walking gently.
