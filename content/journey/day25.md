# Day 25 - Patience in Conflict

## Arrival moment

Pause before reacting.
Not every response needs to be immediate.

"Those who restrain anger and pardon people."

## Opening reflection

Conflict tests character quickly.
Patience in conflict is not weakness.
It is disciplined strength under emotional pressure.

## Seerah moment

When the Prophet (peace be upon him) entered Makkah with power, he chose forgiveness over revenge.
This was moral strength, not passivity.

## Quran reflection

Read with restraint in mind.

3:134
41:34

## Tafsir support

The Quran teaches believers to repel harm with what is better when possible.
This transforms conflict from ego-defense into worship.

## Hadith connection

The Prophet (peace be upon him) said: "The strong one is not the one who overcomes others in wrestling, but the one who controls himself in anger."

## Private reflection

What kind of conflict triggers you most?
What helps you stay principled without becoming harsh?

## Tiny action

In your next tense moment, delay your response by one full breath and ask Allah for calm.

## Closing moment

A restrained heart protects dignity - yours and theirs.
Walk with strength and mercy together.
