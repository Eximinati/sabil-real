# Cross-Language Consistency Rules (Phase 5I)

## Goal

Prevent translation drift so English and Urdu remain the same spiritual journey.

## Non-Negotiables

1. Arc alignment
- `arc_identity` must match between languages.

2. Week identity alignment
- `week_chapter` meaning must remain equivalent.

3. Seerah progression alignment
- Seerah references must remain in same historical/emotional progression.

4. Emotional pacing alignment
- Day should move through similar calm/depth cadence across languages.

## Emotional Equivalence Rules

- Keep same emotional movement, not same sentence structure.
- Preserve invitation quality and contemplative rhythm.
- Preserve beginner safety and spiritual softness.
- Avoid intensity mismatch between languages.

## Drift Warning Signs

- Urdu sounds more formal than English.
- Urdu tone becomes preachy while English stays companion-like.
- One language becomes heavier or denser than the other.
- Seerah emphasis shifts to trivia in one language.
- Reflection prompts diverge emotionally.

## Correction Guidance

When drift is found:
1. Compare intent line-by-line (not syntax).
2. Rewrite for emotional parity.
3. Re-check contemplative pacing.
4. Re-run emotional review and consistency checks.

## Metadata Discipline

Always keep these synchronized:
- translation stage
- source and sync hashes
- drift flags
- cross-language check completion state
