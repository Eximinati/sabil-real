# Localization Editorial Anti-Patterns (Phase 5I)

## Purpose

Protect Sabil from multilingual drift as localization volume grows.

## Anti-Patterns and Corrections

1. Robotic translation
- Warning sign: sentence feels machine-literal, emotionally flat.
- Correction: rewrite for natural emotional intent and rhythm.

2. Over-formality
- Warning sign: bureaucratic, sermon-like, or institutional wording.
- Correction: use companion-like, human, warm language.

3. Emotional flattening
- Warning sign: translation is "correct" but has no softness.
- Correction: restore tenderness and contemplative pauses.

4. Repetitive reassurance loops
- Warning sign: same comforting sentence repeated without progression.
- Correction: vary reassurance while preserving calm identity.

5. Inconsistent Seerah tone
- Warning sign: one language uses lived resonance, another reads as trivia dump.
- Correction: keep Seerah as emotionally meaningful mirror in all languages.

6. Literalism over authenticity
- Warning sign: mirrored syntax but broken emotional equivalence.
- Correction: keep intent, not grammar shape.

7. Harsh localization drift
- Warning sign: localized copy introduces blame, urgency, or spiritual pressure.
- Correction: return to welcome-first, mercy-first framing.

## Quick Review Prompts

- Does this still sound like Sabil?
- Would a spiritually tired beginner feel safe reading this?
- Does this line invite return without guilt?
- Does this preserve contemplative pacing?
