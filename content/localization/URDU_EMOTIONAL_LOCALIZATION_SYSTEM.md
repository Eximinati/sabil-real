# Sabil Urdu Tone and Emotional Localization System (Phase 5D)

## Purpose

Protect emotional soul of Sabil in Urdu.

Urdu in Sabil must feel:
- warm
- spiritually close
- calm
- reflective
- beginner-safe

Urdu in Sabil must not feel:
- robotic translation
- institutional religious messaging
- khutbah lecture
- madrasa textbook
- guilt pressure
- sectarian gatekeeping

Core feeling:
"You are welcome here. Walk gently toward Allah."

---

## 1) Official Urdu Tone Philosophy

### Voice identity
- Speak like trusted gentle companion, not authority podium.
- Keep warmth before instruction.
- Keep sincerity before correctness performance.
- Use modern accessible Urdu, not ornamental literary Urdu.

### Emotional atmosphere
- Reader should feel seen, not judged.
- Reader should feel invited, not pushed.
- Reader should feel safe to return after breaks.
- Reader should feel private spiritual space, not public religious exam.

### Sentence rhythm philosophy
- Prefer short-to-medium lines.
- Use breathing pauses between emotional beats.
- One emotional move per sentence.
- Keep close with soft landing line.

### Spiritual softness rules
- Mention Allah with nearness language, not fear-first pressure.
- Use mercy and return framing before accountability framing.
- Keep instruction gentle and actionable.
- Avoid absolutist correction voice.

## 2) Good vs Bad Urdu Examples

### Onboarding
- Bad: "آپ کو روزانہ دینی اسباق مکمل کرنے چاہئیں۔"
- Better: "ہر دن ایک نرم قدم کافی ہے۔ جہاں سے دل تیار ہو، وہیں سے شروع کریں۔"

### Reflection prompt
- Bad: "اپنی کوتاہیوں کا اعتراف کریں اور اصلاح کریں۔"
- Better: "آج دل کے کس حصے کو اللہ کے سامنے نرمی سے کھولنا چاہتے ہیں؟"

### Return flow
- Bad: "آپ کئی دن پیچھے رہ گئے ہیں۔"
- Better: "آپ واپس آئے، یہی بڑی بات ہے۔ آج سے نرمی سے پھر شروع کریں۔"

### Tiny action
- Bad: "آج کم از کم 20 منٹ لازمی عبادت کریں۔"
- Better: "آج ایک چھوٹا سا عمل چنیں، جو دل سکون سے نبھا سکے۔"

### Reassurance
- Bad: "اللہ تعالیٰ کی طرف رجوع کرنا چاہیے۔"
- Better: "اللہ اب بھی آپ کو اپنی طرف بلاتا ہے۔"

### Quran framing
- Bad: "مندرجہ ذیل آیات کا مطالعہ فرمائیں۔"
- Better: "ان آیات کے ساتھ تھوڑی دیر ٹھہریں۔"

### Navigation wording
- Bad: "منعکسات"
- Better: "تاملات"

## 3) Forbidden Localization Patterns

Never use:
- Dense Arabic insertion where plain Urdu works.
- Literal word-by-word translation from English.
- Bureaucratic formality (e.g., "برائے مہربانی درج ذیل").
- Guilt-heavy pressure (e.g., "اگر سنجیدہ ہیں تو...").
- Sectarian assumptions about reader background.
- Harsh correction tone.
- Productivity Islam language (streak, optimize, target).
- Emotionally cold transactional lines.

Safety rule:
If line can trigger shame spiral for struggling beginner, rewrite.

---

## 4) Urdu Readability Standards

### Line and sentence
- Target 8-18 words per sentence.
- Break long thoughts into 2 sentences.
- One paragraph 1-3 sentences for mobile comfort.

### Vocabulary
- Prefer high-frequency Urdu words.
- Keep Arabic terms only when spiritually necessary.
- If technical Islamic term used, surround with plain Urdu context.

### Visual rhythm
- Avoid dense paragraph walls.
- Keep white-space between emotional sections.
- Keep prompt lines single-focused.

### Punctuation
- Use full stop and question marks sparingly and clearly.
- Avoid semicolon-heavy scholarly cadence.
- Avoid stacked commas that create breathless tone.

---

## 5) Emotional Localization Rules (Equivalence, not Literal)

Localize emotional intent, not syntax.

### Preserve across English -> Urdu
- emotional pace
- tenderness level
- invitation quality
- contemplative stillness
- closing softness

### Adapt in Urdu
- Reorder phrases for natural Urdu flow.
- Replace abstract instruction with felt-language.
- Shift from formal imperative to gentle invitation.
- Keep tone relational with Allah, not procedural.

Bad equivalence target:
- same sentence structure.

Good equivalence target:
- same emotional effect in reader heart.

## 6) Urdu Reflection Prompt Guidelines

### Good prompt traits
- private, gentle, and spiritually honest
- emotionally specific but non-invasive
- opens door, does not interrogate
- invites return to Allah without shame

### Avoid
- therapy-clinical framing
- self-help coaching tone
- command-heavy guilt lines
- emotionally exposing or confrontational wording

### Prompt patterns to use
- "آج دل کہاں تھکا ہوا محسوس کرتا ہے؟"
- "کس بات کو آپ اللہ کے سپرد کرنا چاہتے ہیں؟"
- "آج ایک سچی دعا آپ کے دل میں کیا ہے؟"

## 7) Urdu World-Building Language Rules

All Urdu surfaces must feel one atmosphere.

### Loading states
- calm, patient, non-technical
- example: "آپ کی جگہ تیار ہو رہی ہے..."

### Onboarding
- belonging and ease, not complexity
- avoid feature-dump language

### Bookmarks
- return-with-gentleness framing
- avoid data-archive tone

### Quran discovery
- invitation to pause and sit
- avoid search-engine tone

### Return flows
- no "behind" language
- welcome-back warmth always

### Empty states
- hopeful and soft
- no deficiency framing

## 8) Lightweight Urdu Emotional QA Checklist

Before publish, check:
1. Warmth: does line feel human and close?
2. Softness: any pressure, blame, or shame hints?
3. Readability: easy on mobile in one pass?
4. Breathability: enough spacing and rhythm?
5. Robotic tone: any literal translation smell?
6. Institutional tone: any sermon/admin language?
7. Beginner safety: safe for emotionally fragile reader?
8. Spiritual intimacy: does line invite heart toward Allah?
9. Coherence: does this match Sabil atmosphere?
10. Return energy: does user feel safe to come back tomorrow?

If two or more fail -> rewrite required.
