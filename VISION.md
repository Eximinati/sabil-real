## The Curriculum Approach: Seerah as the Narrative Spine

Unlike traditional Islamic education that teaches Quran and Hadith in isolation, Sabil weaves them together through **Seerah**—the life of Prophet Muhammad ﷺ. This creates a coherent narrative where Quranic verses come alive with historical context.

### Why Seerah-First?

The Quran was revealed over 23 years in response to real events. Studying verses without their story is like reading the middle chapters of a novel. When we learn Quran through Seerah:

- **Surah Al-Fatiha** becomes meaningful when we understand how early Muslims recited it secretly in Makkah and later proclaimed it openly in Madinah
- **Verses about patience** take on depth when we see Abu Bakr living them during the boycott of Muslims
- **Community verses** become concrete when we study the Charter of Madinah

This approach makes Quran feel less like a textbook and more like a living conversation with Allah.

### Non-Madhab Foundation

Sabil takes a **universal, foundational approach** to Islamic knowledge:

**What we teach:**
- Quran with full contextual understanding
- Seerah as living history
- Authentic Hadith from verified collections
- Tafsir based on early scholarly consensus
- Spiritual development and moral growth
- Practical application of Islamic principles

**What we do not teach in Phase 1-2:**
- Fiqh rulings and madhab-specific jurisprudence
- Detailed rulings on prayer, food, purification
- Advanced jurisprudential debates

**Why?**
- Beginners and reverts should not need to choose a madhab before understanding foundational Islam
- Quran and Seerah belong to all Muslims—universal truths that transcend schools of thought
- Madhab study deserves dedicated, careful treatment as a future expansion (Phase 3)

### Future: Madhab Education

After completing the foundational journey, learners can explore madhab-specific paths:

- **Hanafi** — Structured, comprehensive jurisprudence
- **Shafi'i** — Detailed usul and practical rulings
- **Maliki** — Emphasis on community practice and local consensus
- **Hanbali** — Strong hadith foundation and Athari aqidah

This allows learners to understand differences with respect and make informed decisions about personal practice.

## The Philosophy That Drives Us

Sabil is built on a conviction that Islamic education has strayed too far from its purpose.

For too long, we have measured Islamic learning by how much content we consume—books read, lectures watched, videos saved. We have confused *exposure* with *transformation*.

**Knowledge without reflection is trivia.**
**Information without application is noise.**
**Learning without transformation is wasted time.**

Sabil believes that Islamic education should:

- **Transform hearts, not just transfer information.** The goal is not to memorize more verses—it is to let those verses change how you live.
- **Guide people gently.** Nobody becomes a scholar in a day. Growth takes time, patience, and consistent effort. We meet learners where they are.
- **Create intentional growth.** Not everything at once—structured, meaningful progression that builds on itself. One day builds on the last.
- **Connect knowledge with daily life.** What good is understanding Quran if it never affects how you speak to your family, how you handle stress, how you make decisions?
- **Make people feel spiritually seen.** Not judged. Not compared. Not overwhelmed. Seen. Guided. Supported on a journey that is deeply personal and profoundly meaningful.

### What We're Building Toward

We imagine a Muslim opening Sabil in the morning, reading a reflection prompt that makes them pause and think—really think—about where they are in their relationship with Allah. Then opening a lesson that feels relevant to that struggle. Then closing the lesson with a question they will carry through their day. Then finding themselves naturally remembering that question while driving, working, caring for family.

That is transformation.

That is what Sabil is designed to create.

**Not content consumption. Guided transformation.**
